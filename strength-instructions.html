Strength & Supersets

6-week cycle · 5 training days · heavy compounds + high-volume supersets + AMRAP finishers

✍️ Designed by Mike Cross
How It Works
Split: Five training days per week — Legs · Chest · Back & Shoulders · Arms & Forearms · Calves & Cardio. Hit each day once, then repeat.
The cycle: a 6-week block. Every week keeps the same daily shape, but the exercises and rep schemes rotate underneath you — so no two weeks feel the same while the stimulus stays on target.
The goal: blend raw strength with hypertrophy. You open heavy and low-rep to build the base, then chase volume through supersets and finish every day with an AMRAP burnout.
The Daily Shape · 6 Slots
1
Heavy Compound
🏋️ Low Rep · 4–8 reps
Big lift, heavy load. Full rest between sets — quality over speed.
2
Heavy Compound
🏋️ Low Rep · 4–8 reps
Second heavy movement, strength focus. At least one of slots 1–3 is a pyramid.
3
Heavy Compound / Pyramid
📈 Pyramid · e.g. 8,6,4,4
Climb in weight as reps drop. Builds to a heavy top set.
4
Superset 1
⚡ Pyramid + High Volume · 12–30 reps
Two moves back to back, no rest between. A = pyramid · B = high-volume pump.
5
Superset 2
⚡ Reverse Pyramid + High Volume · 12–30 reps
Same back-to-back format. A often runs a reverse pyramid (reps ↑ / weight ↓).
6
AMRAP Finisher
💀 Burnout · 50–100+ reps
One movement, as many reps as possible. Empty the tank — chase the rep target to failure.
Set Styles & Badge Key
⚡ Superset
Two exercises back to back, no rest between.
📈 Pyramid
Weight increases as reps decrease (12,10,8,8). Reverse pyramid flips it.
🏋️ Low Rep
Heavy load, low reps, strength focus (5×5, 6×4).
🔥 High Rep
Pump-focused, high volume (4×20, 3×30).
💀 AMRAP
As many reps as possible — push the finisher to failure.
The 5 Training Days
🦵
Legs
Quads · hamstrings · glutes · calves. Squats, RDLs, presses → superset accessories → walking-lunge / curl burnout.
💪
Chest
Presses and fly variations across angles → superset pump work → push-up / pec burnout.
🔙
Back & Shoulders
Rows and pulls paired with delt work → rear-delt & lateral supersets → shrug / row AMRAP.
💥
Arms & Forearms
Biceps, triceps and grip — heavy curls and extensions → superset arm blasts → preacher / skull AMRAP.
🏃
Calves & Cardio
High-volume calf work plus conditioning to close the week. Pull a session from the Conditioning Corner.
⏱️ Add Tempo · Optional Intensifier
Tempo is not prescribed here — it's a serviceable add-in. If a slot feels too easy, slow the lift to cut reps and raise output. Tap the ⋮ on any exercise → Add Tempo.
Read it as Eccentric : Pause bottom : Concentric : Pause top. Example: 3:0:1:0 = 3-sec lower, explosive lift. 2:0:1:2 = 2-sec lower, 2-sec squeeze at the top.
Week-by-Week · The 6-Week Cycle
Across all six weeks the daily shape stays fixed — 3 heavy compounds, 2 supersets, 1 finisher. What rotates is the exercise selection and the rep schemes, so you keep adapting without ever leaving the framework.
Week 1Establish
Learn the lifts, set your working weights, and log your AMRAP rep targets as a baseline.
Week 2Build
New exercise mix. Add load to the heavy slots where last week felt controlled.
Week 3Intensify
Fresh pyramids and supersets. Tighten rest on the supersets; push the finisher higher.
Week 4Push
Heaviest compound work of the block, with a high-rep finisher to match.
Week 5Peak Volume
Volume rises across the supersets. Consider adding tempo to the slots that feel easy.
Week 6Max Out
Top of the cycle — heaviest schemes paired with the biggest AMRAP targets. Beat your Week 1 numbers, then restart the cycle.
